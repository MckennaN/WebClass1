All-American Girls Professional Baseball League

Innovations

  1. This team is the cause for ten women to go to the Hall of Fame.
  2. This league was the first all women's league of its time.
  3. The All-American Girls Professional Baseball League was the first women's team to claim the name 'Baseball League', instead of softball.

History

The title 'All-American Girls Professional Baseball League' or AAGPBL, was not used at first, but rather concerning the topic of softball. The group had started in 1943, by Philip K. Wrigley, oddly enough a man. The league consisted of eager females who would die for the sport, thus the team's saying "Do or Die". The team died down back in 1954, but it will be remembered in the Hall of Fame.
